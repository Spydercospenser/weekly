Why These Rules Work

The QML243 rules capitalize on institutional trading patterns that repeat weekly. Large players establish positions early in the week (Monday-Tuesday), test levels mid-week (Wednesday), and then follow through or reverse based on the market's reaction to these tests. The rules provide a framework to anticipate these moves.

This strategy works because it aligns with how institutional traders operate. They typically:

  1. Establish positions early in the week (Monday-Tuesday)
  2. Test liquidity and reactions mid-week (Wednesday)
  3. Adjust positions based on market reactions (Thursday)
  4. Square positions before the weekend (Friday)

Implementing the QML243 Strategy

Weekly Preparation

Begin each week by analyzing the higher time frame context (weekly/monthly charts). Identify key support/resistance levels, trend direction, and any significant economic events scheduled for the week. This establishes the broader context for your trades.

Monday Analysis

Monitor how price develops relative to the weekly open. Watch for LOTW (Low of the Week) formation. The relationship between Monday's high and Tuesday's high will set up your Wednesday trade. Track volume patterns carefully - institutional participation often reveals itself early in the week.

Tuesday Confirmation

Tuesday serves as confirmation day. Compare Tuesday's high with Monday's high to determine your Wednesday bias. Watch for manipulation around Monday's range extremes. The close position (especially in the last hour) often provides strong clues about Wednesday's direction.

Understanding Institutional Weekly Flow

Professional traders operate on weekly cycles because their performance is often measured weekly. The QML243 strategy exploits this by identifying:

  • Positioning Phase (Monday-Tuesday): Institutions establish their weekly positions, creating the initial range.
  • Testing Phase (Wednesday): They test reactions at key levels to determine if their positions are valid.
  • Adjustment Phase (Thursday): Based on Wednesday's reaction, they either add to or reduce positions.
  • Liquidation Phase (Friday): Positions are squared before the weekend to reduce risk exposure.

This predictable flow creates recurring patterns that the QML243 strategy systematically trades.

Advanced Daily Tactics

Beyond the basic daily patterns, professional traders watch for these subtle cues:

  • Monday Gaps: Gaps often fill by Wednesday, creating trading opportunities
  • Tuesday Traps: False breakouts on Tuesday often reverse Wednesday
  • Wednesday News: Economic releases often catalyze expected reversals
  • Thursday Options: Options expiry influences late-week price action
  • Friday Squeezes: Thin liquidity can create exaggerated moves

The QML243 strategy incorporates filters for these patterns to improve trade accuracy.

Advanced Risk Techniques

Professional traders using QML243 often employ these advanced risk management tactics:

  • Volatility Adjustments: Increase position size when volatility is contracting ahead of expected expansions (Wednesday)
  • News Filters: Reduce size or avoid trading around high-impact news events that can disrupt patterns
  • Correlation Hedging: Use correlated instruments to hedge during uncertain periods (Thursday-Friday)
  • Time-Based Exits: Close all positions by Friday close regardless of profit/loss to avoid weekend risk
  • Weekly Drawdown Limits: Set strict weekly loss limits (e.g., 5%) to prevent emotional trading

These techniques help professional traders maintain consistency with the QML243 strategy.
